Battle of Didgori

About the Event

The Battle of Didgori was fought between the Kingdom of Georgia and the Seljuk Turks on August 12, 1121. It was a decisive victory for the Georgians, led by King David IV, also known as David the Builder.

The battle took place near the Didgori Valley in present-day Georgia and marked a turning point in the history of the region. It solidified Georgia's independence and expanded its territories.
